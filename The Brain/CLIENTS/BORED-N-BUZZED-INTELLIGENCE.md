## BRAND GAPS & SIGNAL OPPORTUNITIES

**1. They want to be a lifestyle brand but the site is still a deal sheet.**
Their own language says "more than just a dispensary" and "laid-back destination." The Jim Jones event proves they can pull culture. But the homepage is dominated by daily deal banners. The lifestyle story isn't being told consistently.

**2. "Stay One Puff Ahead" is an excellent tagline that disappears after the hero.**
It appears once at the top and never again. It should be the backbone of every content pillar.

**3. The community angle is untapped.**
They serve Kirkland, Woodinville, Redmond, Bothell. There's a "Need A Buzz? Things to do in Kirkland" blog concept on the blog page that was never developed. Local community content is a massive organic growth lever they haven't pulled.

**4. The rewards program is strong but barely marketed.**
30% off first visit, birthday deals, social media discounts — this is a powerful acquisition engine. It's buried on an interior page. It should be leading content.

**5. Social proof is missing.**
They incentivize Google reviews (25% off) and social posts (25% off) but there's no visible social feed, review wall, or UGC anywhere on the site. The currency is there. The showcase isn't.

**6. The blog has no publishing cadence.**
Four posts over 6 months. Educational content is a strong SEO play for a cannabis shop but needs consistency and a clear editorial voice.

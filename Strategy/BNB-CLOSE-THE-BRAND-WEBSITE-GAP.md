## The Four Moves (In Order)

### Move 1 — Fix the Contact Page Bug
**Time to execute:** 20 minutes
**What:** The `/contact/` page is live with WordPress template placeholder data — "Urban Jungle Co." at a fake Illinois address. Full details in `Notes/URGENT-contact-page-bug.md`.
**Why first:** It's an embarrassment on the production site. Fix it before driving any traffic. Confirm real email + social handles with client, then update in WordPress/Elementor.

---

### Move 2 — Build "Need A Buzz? Things to Do in Kirkland"
**Pillar:** The Community
**Format:** Blog post, 800–1,200 words
**Why this post:**
- It was already sitting as a concept on their blog page — the headline exists, the post was never written
- It's the piece that plants B&B as a Kirkland cultural institution, not just a shop
- Uncle Ike's can never write this post authentically. A Seattle chain has no claim on Kirkland neighborhood identity.
- Hyper-local lifestyle content is strong organic SEO — people search "things to do in Kirkland" constantly
- It opens the Community pillar and creates a content template that can be repeated (things to do near Juanita Beach, Totem Lake Town Center, etc.)

**Post structure:**
- Intro: You just stopped by Bored N Buzzed. The vibe is good. Now what?
- 5–7 Kirkland activities with personality-driven copy (waterfront, Juanita Beach, local food, music, art)
- Each tied back to B&B's energy — laid-back, curious, community-first
- CTA: Come back and tell us what you did. Tag us. (Rewards plug.)

---

### Move 3 — Write the Jim Jones Recap as the Signature Culture Post
**Pillar:** The Culture
**Format:** Blog post / event recap, 600–1,000 words
**Why this post:**
- The event already happened. The photos exist (Jimm-Jones-1.png, 2.png, 3.png on the site).
- The story is already written in draft form on the blog — it needs to be elevated to full B&B voice
- This is the permanent proof that B&B is a culture brand. It lives on the site forever and gets referenced every time someone asks "who are these guys?"
- It establishes the events/culture content category with a flagship piece before any future events

**Post structure:**
- Open on the moment — what it felt like in the room, not just what happened
- Jim Jones context (Dipset, *We Fly High*, cultural weight)
- The B&B angle — why this event, why Kirkland, what it means for the community
- The night: venue, vendor partners (Sky High + WYLD), merch drop, Mirra afterparty
- Close: "This is just the beginning." — forward-looking, builds anticipation
- Pull quotes from the existing on-site recap copy and elevate them

---

### Move 4 — Establish the 60-Day Content Calendar
**Pillar:** All five
**Format:** Strategic document → `Strategy/BNB-60-DAY-CONTENT-CALENDAR.md`
**Why:**
- 4 posts in 6 months is not a brand. It's a ghost town.
- 2–4 posts per month across all five pillars is the engine that turns the site into a living brand
- The Education pillar drives SEO (Kirkland cannabis dispensary searches)
- The Culture pillar drives identity and social sharing
- The Deal pillar drives conversion and foot traffic

**Target cadence:** 2 posts per month minimum to start. Rotate pillars. Education is the SEO anchor. Culture is the identity anchor.
